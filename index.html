Beb El Rezk

Directed by Heifel Ben Youssef (2024)

After an exile of 23 years, Slim returns to Tunisia to bury his father and resume work on the family farm. He discovers that his first love, Narjes, is married to his father's partner, Hajj Taher, a man with a fortune built on corruption and exploitation of workers, who runs the business with an iron hand and controls his three children. "Beb El Rezk" delves into the folds of rural and urban life, while addressing current topics embodied in the hostility between Hajj Taher and Slim.

Who's Next ?

"By mistake? The child not embraced by his homeland will burn it down to feel its warmth" was the title of another special "tableau" at the end of JCC, which partly tells the story of resistance in "Acces Denied" and partly reflects our life in a country that hates us as much as we hate it. They wanted to shape us in their own way, but we are determined to live our lives on our terms.

JCC's opening

"We will become what we want to be, the journey has just begun, and the path is not yet complete" (-Mahmud Darwish) was the title of the poster that summarized the story of the youngest video editor in the official competition of the Carthage Film Festival 2022 (at least in the feature films category). It all started in 2019 as a trainee camera cinematographer, then in 2020 as a trainee video editor, and in 2021 also, step by step, a film edited by me made it to the official competition in 2022 with a special mention from the jury.

Film Studies Graduation

Different from the usual, I wanted to break away from the classic graduation scene with caps and gowns, and I decided to represent three years of hard work in a way that reflects my passion. That's why I chose a simulation of an editing software,specifically the timeline part. The story began during the orientation period when I heard various opinions like, "Why study cinema? Does anyone even study cinema? Why don't you do something practical and make money?" DO YOU THINK A SANE PERSON WOULD DO THIS JOB ? In this world, I turned a deaf ear to these opinions. In my timeline, I muted them and continued my editing. Each segment and each track represented one of the 3 years I spent in film school, and every year had its significance, its lessons, and its learning. And because "workflow" is considered one of the most crucial aspects of our work, we added the project, The "Video Editor Project" source. And then, we concluded with the final step : EXPORTING. Till today, I don't consider myself a pro designer or a master in editing because it's all about hardwork, the mindset, and the idea represented in the details of each "tableau" and each video. They are our identity, and what remains of technique and aesthetics comes with time.
